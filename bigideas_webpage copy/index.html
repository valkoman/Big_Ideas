The Big Ideas Grant

The Whittier Alliance BIG IDEAS Grant provides funding of up to $4,000 for any creative endeavor that gives back to the Whittier neighborhood.

Any individual, business, student group, non-profit organization, or community group that proposes to do good work and have a meaningful impact in the Whittier neighborhood is eligible to receive funding.

Charlie and Me, Whittier 2008

Motivation

As a former resident of Whittier, I’ve got a soft spot in my heart for the neighborhood. Whittier is where I started a family and learned to walk the design tightrope at MCAD.

So when the opportunity was announced to partner with the Whittier Alliance to produce a BIG IDEAS campaign, it was a no- brainer.

Moodboard detail / Ionic column

How do you solve a problem like BIG IDEAS?

With the BIG IDEAS grant still in its infancy, the Whittier Alliance is making a push to get the word out. But how?

My strategy: to highlght the diversity of the neighborhood through its architecture, and to show civic engagement by using photos of people working + building.

The constraints: limited visual content + a small budget.

How do you catch a cloud and pin it down?

Process + Tools

My design process is simple: Sit in darkness, chant. Commune with the creative spirit, expel demons! Stay hydrated, exercise. Pencil, cut, paper + paste. Repeat. Surf the web, chat with groundskeeper. Back to darkness, chant more. Adobe!

Prototype + Ideation

My initial strategy was to use architectural elements from the Whittier neighborhood, along with colored squares. All images are rendered into bitmap Photoshop files that are then colored in Indesign.

The Ionic column taken from the Minneapolis Museum of Art (the heart of Whittier), resides within the logo mark.

"Very nice! Where are the people?"

Fold-out pamphlet / cut + paste mock-ups (various papers)

Feedback

Initial feedback focused on the need to include elements from Whitttier: photos of neighborhood, residents working + building.

The overall concept of a fold-out flier/poster was well received, though the somewhat menacing aesthetic needed to be toned down.

"A little less death metal, a little more civic engagement."

Flibbertigibbet!

Revised prototype

Prototype Revisions

Going back to the drawing board, a new path forward emerged. One with more light and people! Though my revisions were met with praise, work still needed to be done.

"Design feels like a museum brochure. Make it more inline with community action, non-profit work!"

Images courtesy of the Whittier Alliance. All rights reserved.

Images

The Whittier Alliance provided much needed imagery that really turned the project around. I was given 800+ images to choose from, which I narrowed down to a handful of healthy prospects.

Most of the images were taken from the Whittier Alliance's annual street party, making the task of finding appropriate imagery a little difficult.

After selecting a robust representation of Whittier's finest, I went ahead and applied the newly rendered images into the design.

Protoype, Second Round

After receiving further feedback, my direction was set. Design elements from a new and revised moodboard helped articulate the updated design and a new path forward.

Discussion centered on the overall usefullness of piece — was this something purely to get the message out, or could it serve a greater purpose for the residents of Whittier?

A form that can be filled out and dropped in a postbox was added to the fold-out poster for the neighborhood to submit their BIG IDEAS.

Logo / wordmark

After further consideration (and a broken heart), the "Ionic" logo/wordmark was replaced by a clean, stacked serif.

The logo/wordmark can be used as a solitary element and as a repetitive, rhythmic thread running throughout campaign.

Prototype mock-ups / Second round

Final Campaign

After finalizing the overall feel of the campaign, the design still needed to simplified. Much of the decisions made in the previous prototypes remained, but the presentation needed to be cleaner, more precise, direct.

Summary

And with all being said and done, there is still more work to do.

The next steps: Determine a soft launch to get the BIG IDEAS messaging out into the neighborhood. To build upon the strong start set forth by the grant, and to promote + celebrate the diversity found within Whittier.

Stay tuned for more in 2019!
